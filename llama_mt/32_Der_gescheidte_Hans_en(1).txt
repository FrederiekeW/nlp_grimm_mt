Hans takes the goat, ties its legs and puts it in the pocket. As he comes home, it is suffocated. ‘Good evening, Mother.’ ‘Good evening, Hans. ‘Where are you off to, Hans?’ ‘To Grethel, Mother.’ ‘Do well, Hans.’ ‘I’ll do well. Goodbye, Mother.’ ‘Goodbye, Hans.’

Hans goes to Grethel: ‘Good day, Grethel.’ ‘Good day, Hans. What good things do you bring?’ ‘I bring nothing, I give.’ Grethel gives Hans a piece of bacon. ‘Goodbye, Grethel.’ ‘Goodbye, Hans.’

Hans takes the bacon, ties it to a rope and pulls it behind him. The dogs come and eat the bacon. As he comes home, he has the rope in his hand and nothing is left on it. ‘Good evening, Mother.’ ‘Good evening, Hans. Where have you been?’ ‘I was with Grethel.’ ‘What did you bring her?’ ‘I brought nothing, I gave.’ ‘What did Grethel give you?’ ‘A piece of bacon.’ ‘Where is the bacon, Hans?’ ‘I tied it to a rope, led it home, and chased the dogs away.’ ‘You did that foolishly, Hans, you should have carried the bacon on your head.’ ‘It doesn’t matter, I’ll do better.’

‘Where are you off to, Hans?’ ‘To Grethel, Mother.’ ‘Do well, Hans.’ ‘I’ll do well. Goodbye, Mother.’ ‘Goodbye, Hans.’

Hans goes to Grethel: ‘Good day, Grethel.’ ‘Good day, Hans. What good things do you bring?’ ‘I bring nothing, I give.’ Grethel gives Hans a calf. ‘Goodbye, Grethel.’ ‘Goodbye, Hans.’

Hans takes the calf, puts it on his head, and the calf tramples his face. ‘Good evening, Mother.’ ‘Good evening, Hans.’ ‘Where have you been?’ ‘I was with Grethel.’ ‘What did you bring her?’ ‘I brought nothing, I gave.’ ‘What did Grethel give you?’ ‘A calf.’ ‘Where is the calf, Hans?’ ‘I put it on my head, and it trampled my face.’ ‘You did that foolishly, Hans, you should have led the calf, and tied it to the fence.’ ‘It doesn’t matter, I’ll do better.’

‘Where are you off to, Hans?’ ‘To Grethel, Mother.’ ‘Do well, Hans.’ ‘I’ll do well. Goodbye, Mother.’ ‘Goodbye, Hans.’

Hans goes to Grethel: ‘Good day, Grethel.’ ‘Good day, Hans. What good things do you bring?’ ‘I bring nothing, I give.’ Grethel says to Hans ‘I want to go with you.’

Hans takes Grethel, ties her to a rope, leads her, takes her to the fence and ties her fast. Then Hans goes to his mother. ‘Good evening, Mother.’ ‘Good evening, Hans. Where have you been?’ ‘I was with Grethel.’ ‘What did you bring her?’ ‘I brought nothing.’ ‘What did Grethel give you?’ ‘Nothing, I went with her.’ ‘Where did you leave Grethel?’ ‘I led her on a rope, tied her to the fence, and threw grass at her.’ ‘You did that foolishly, Hans, you should have given her friendly eyes.’ ‘It doesn’t matter, I’ll do better.’

Hans goes into the stable, stabs all the calves and sheep in the eyes, and throws them in Grethel’s face. Then Grethel becomes angry, breaks free and runs away, and was Hans’s bride.
